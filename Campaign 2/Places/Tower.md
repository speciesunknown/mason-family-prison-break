# Tower of Elder Mages

## First Floor - The Extradimensional Lobby
An MC Escher-inspired space with impossible geometry, extradimensional space, and talking paintings. This floor provides access to various rooms and locations throughout the tower (and beyond).

**Features:**
- Stairs that lead both up and down simultaneously
- Doors that open to different locations depending on which direction you approach
- Talking paintings that serve as guides, guards, or sources of information
- Rooms that appear larger on the inside than the outside
- Gravity that shifts based on which "wall" you're standing on

**Current Status:**
- Low-level yugoloths have taken over the tower
- Many of the talking paintings have been corrupted or silenced by the yugoloths
- The extradimensional spaces are being used as hideouts and staging areas
- Some rooms are trapped or guarded by yugoloth patrols

## Accessible Locations via First Floor

### The Infinite Library
- Accessed through a painting of a library that becomes a portal
- Contains books from across all planes and timelines
- The books rearrange themselves based on what you're looking for
- Some books are sentient and will argue with readers
- Contains restricted sections accessible only with proper authorization

### BoomBoom's Laboratory
- Currently partially phased out of reality due to magical accident
- Contains experimental spell components and magical devices
- The reality stabilization device is located here
- Accessible through a door that flickers between existence and non-existence

### The Observatory of Planes
- A room with windows that look out onto different planes of existence
- Can be used to observe other realities, but viewing is dangerous
- Contains scrying equipment and planar communication devices
- One window is permanently broken, leaking planar energy

### The Temporal Archive
- A room where time flows differently
- Contains records of events that haven't happened yet
- Visitors must be careful not to stay too long or risk temporal displacement
- Accessed through a clock face on the wall

### The Elemental Forge
- A workshop where magical items are crafted using elemental forces
- Contains forges connected to the Elemental Planes
- Dangerous but essential for high-level magical crafting
- Accessible through a door that appears to be made of fire

### The Memory Vault
- A secure location where important memories are stored in crystal form
- Used by mages to preserve knowledge or remove traumatic memories
- **Note:** Shadow's first year of life is actually in the possession of Knickers (the arcanaloth), not stored here
- Accessible only through solving a puzzle with the talking paintings
- Currently guarded by yugoloths who are searching for valuable memories

### The Garden of Thought
- An extradimensional garden where ideas grow as physical plants
- Concepts can be harvested and used in spell research
- Some thoughts are dangerous and must be handled carefully
- Accessed through a painting of a garden that becomes real

### The Chamber of Reflection
- A room with mirrors that show different versions of yourself
- Can be used for divination and self-discovery
- Some mirrors show possible futures or alternate realities
- One mirror is cracked and shows distorted reflections

### The Archive of Lost Spells
- Contains spells that have been forgotten or banned
- Protected by magical wards and guardian constructs
- Some spells are sentient and try to escape
- Accessible through a door that only appears when you're looking for something specific

### The Guest Quarters
- Extradimensional rooms for visiting mages
- Each room can be customized by the occupant's will
- Some rooms have been abandoned and contain forgotten experiments
- Currently housing various mages, including potentially Mr Fuckles the Cabbagineer

### The Kitchen of Culinary Magic
- A magical kitchen where food can be enchanted
- Contains ingredients from across the planes
- Some dishes have magical effects when consumed
- Possibly where Mr Fuckles works on his cabbage alchemy

### The Vault of Artifacts
- Secure storage for powerful magical items
- Protected by multiple layers of magical and physical security
- Contains items too dangerous to be left unguarded
- Access requires multiple mages' authorization

### The Meditation Chambers
- Rooms designed for magical meditation and spell preparation
- Time flows differently here, allowing extended preparation
- Some chambers are attuned to specific schools of magic
- Can be used to recover spell slots faster

### The Portal Nexus
- A central hub connecting to various portals throughout the multiverse
- Requires proper authorization and knowledge to use safely
- Some portals are unstable or lead to dangerous locations
- The talking paintings can provide directions but charge a fee
- Currently being used by yugoloths to bring in reinforcements from Gehenna

### The Controller's Chamber
- Contains the Controller from Campaign 1 (big-brained troll with upside-down head)
- The Controller has been relocated here, possibly by the yugoloths or for other purposes
- Controls security systems and magical defenses throughout the tower
- The room contains various control mechanisms and monitoring devices
- Access requires bypassing security systems that the Controller manages
- The Controller may or may not remember previous encounters depending on its memory status

## Yugoloth Encounters

### Low-Level Yugoloth Types Present:
- **[Mezzoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth)** - Warrior yugoloths serving as guards and patrols
- **[Nycaloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17125-nycaloth)** - Flying scouts and messengers
- **[Canoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17098-canoloth)** - Trackers hunting for specific targets or items
- **[Yagnoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17101-yagnoloth)** - Mercenaries hired to secure the tower
- **[Dhergoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17097-dhergoloth)** - Brutish destroyers used for heavy combat

### Encounter Locations:

**First Floor Lobby:**
- 1d4+1 [mezzoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth) patrolling the extradimensional space
- Some talking paintings have been replaced with yugoloth-controlled ones that give false directions
- Yugoloths use the impossible geometry to their advantage in combat

**The Infinite Library:**
- 2-3 [canoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17098-canoloth) tracking down specific books or information
- Books have been rearranged to hide important texts
- Some sections are booby-trapped by yugoloths

**The Observatory of Planes:**
- 1 [nycaloth](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17125-nycaloth) using the observatory to communicate with other yugoloths
- The broken window is being used as an entry point for more yugoloths
- Planar communication devices have been repurposed for yugoloth use

**The Elemental Forge:**
- 2 [mezzoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth) attempting to forge weapons or items
- The forge's connection to elemental planes has been corrupted
- Dangerous elemental creatures may be accidentally summoned

**The Archive of Lost Spells:**
- 1 [yagnoloth](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17101-yagnoloth) mercenary guarding the entrance
- Yugoloths are attempting to steal or corrupt powerful spells
- Some spells have been released and are now hostile

**The Guest Quarters:**
- Various yugoloths using abandoned rooms as hideouts
- 1d3+1 yugoloths per room, resting or planning
- Some rooms contain prisoners or captured mages

**The Vault of Artifacts:**
- Heavy yugoloth presence (3-5 [mezzoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth) + 1 [yagnoloth](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17101-yagnoloth) leader)
- Yugoloths are attempting to break into the vault
- Security systems are failing due to yugoloth interference

**The Portal Nexus:**
- 2 [nycaloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/nycaloth) maintaining portal connections
- Regular yugoloth reinforcements arriving through portals
- Portal to Gehenna is active and guarded

**BoomBoom's Laboratory:**
- The broken reality makes this area dangerous for yugoloths too
- 1-2 yugoloths trapped in the distorted reality, now hostile
- Yugoloths avoid this area due to the magical instability

**The Controller's Chamber:**
- 2 [mezzoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth) guarding the Controller
- The Controller may be under yugoloth control or working with them
- Security systems throughout the tower are compromised

## Wandering Encounters

### Encounter 1: The Scholar and His Apprentices
**Location:** Can appear in The Infinite Library, The Archive of Lost Spells, or The Temporal Archive

**Encounter:**
- **1 [Mezzoloth](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth) (CR 5)** - "The Librarian" - A mezzoloth tracking down specific magical texts, accompanied by its interns
- **3-4 [Imps](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/16856-imp) (CR 1 each)** - Interns carrying books and scrolls, taking notes
- **1-2 [Quasits](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/16923-quasit) (CR 1 each)** - Interns responsible for organizing stolen knowledge

**Tactics:**
- The mezzoloth uses teleportation to control the battlefield and hunt down party members
- Imps use invisibility to flank and harass from multiple angles
- Quasits provide support with their scare ability and shapechanging
- The mezzoloth will try to capture rather than kill if it thinks the party has useful information

**Loot:** Stolen spell scrolls, research notes about the tower's defenses, a map of yugoloth patrol routes

---

### Encounter 2: The Brute Squad
**Location:** Can appear in The Elemental Forge, The Vault of Artifacts, or corridors

**Encounter:**
- **1 [Dhergoloth](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17097-dhergoloth) (CR 7)** - "The Foreman" - A brutish yugoloth overseeing destruction and looting
- **2 [Mezzoloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth) (CR 5 each)** - Warrior interns following orders
- **1-2 [Magmin](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/16880-magmin) (CR 1/2 each)** - Elemental interns from the forge, serving as living tools

**Tactics:**
- Dhergoloth charges into melee, using its destructive abilities
- Mezzoloths provide ranged support with their teleportation
- Magmin explode when killed, dealing fire damage
- The group works as a coordinated unit, with the dhergoloth giving orders

**Loot:** Partially forged weapons, stolen magical components, a list of artifacts the yugoloths are trying to steal

---

### Encounter 3: The Scout Patrol
**Location:** Can appear in The Observatory of Planes, The Portal Nexus, or The First Floor Lobby

**Encounter:**
- **1 [Mezzoloth](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth) (CR 5)** - "The Scout Master" - A mezzoloth coordinating reconnaissance and patrols
- **1 [Mezzoloth](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17099-mezzoloth) (CR 5)** - Ground support intern
- **3-4 [Sprites](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/17018-sprite) (CR 1/4 each)** - Fey interns captured and forced to serve as messengers/scouts

**Tactics:**
- Both mezzoloths use teleportation to control positioning and cut off escape routes
- Mezzoloths coordinate attacks, teleporting to flank party members
- Sprites use their heart sight to detect party members' emotions and intentions, reporting back
- The scout master will try to escape if reduced to half HP to report to superiors

**Loot:** Intelligence reports about the party, portal activation keys, a communication device linking to other yugoloth groups

**Note:** These encounters can be triggered by:
- Rolling a d20 when the party enters a new area (1-3 = encounter)
- After a certain amount of time exploring
- When the party makes too much noise or uses obvious magic
- As a random patrol that discovers the party
