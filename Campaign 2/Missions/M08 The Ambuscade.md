# The Ambuscade

Klevane's original mission was to lure the party into an ambush. Now that he's had a change of heart — after the party descended into the underworld to save his soul — he's willing to share everything he knows.

## Setup

Klevane reveals that the ambush was ordered by **[Dirge](../NPCs/Dirge.md)**, his former mentor — a half-elf brute with a barbarian's rage and the charisma to command fanatic loyalty. Klevane had been following Dirge's orders from the start.

Klevane shares the details of the planned ambush:
- [Dirge](../NPCs/Dirge.md) is the one running it
- Where it was supposed to take place
- How many enemies are involved and what forces they have
- What the signal was supposed to be

### Enemy Forces

- **[Dirge](../NPCs/Dirge.md)** — Gang leader. Half-elf barbarian. CR 9. The most dangerous combatant on the field.
- **[Needle](../NPCs/Needle.md)** — Scout. Wood elf. CR 5. Positioned ahead of the kill zone, responsible for the signal. Passive Perception 18.
- **[Vael Crookedbow](../NPCs/Vael%20Crookedbow.md)** — Lieutenant. Half-elf. CR 5. Commands the main body of ambushers in the kill zone.
- **[Thornwick](../NPCs/Thornwick.md)** — Druid. Wood elf. CR 4. Battlefield control from the treeline. Potential crack in enemy loyalty.
- Assorted bandits (use **Bandit** and **Bandit Captain** stat blocks as needed)

### Prisoners in Camp

If the party raids Dirge's camp (or the ambush leads them there), they may find:
- **[Maren Soldoval](../NPCs/Maren%20Soldoval.md)** — Half-elf merchant. Knows the camp layout, has heard the gang discuss the ambush.
- **[Old Ferris](../NPCs/Old%20Ferris.md)** — Human farmer. Knows guard rotations, daily routine, and the tension between Vael and Thornwick.

The party now has the rare advantage of knowing an ambush is coming. They can choose how to use this information.

## Possible Approaches

### Turn the Trap
Use Klevane's intel to set up a counter-ambush. Let the enemy think everything is going to plan, then spring a trap of their own.

### Avoid It Entirely
Take a different route or change plans to sidestep the ambush altogether. Safer, but the enemy remains in play.

### Feed Misinformation
Have Klevane report back that the party is on schedule, but feed false details about their numbers, route, or timing to draw the ambushers into a disadvantageous position.

### Confront Dirge
Skip the ambush entirely and go straight for Dirge, using Klevane's knowledge to find him.

## Complications

- Can Klevane be fully trusted? His change of heart seems genuine, but [Dirge](../NPCs/Dirge.md)'s influence ran deep
- Dirge will have contingencies if Klevane goes silent or is late reporting in
- [Needle](../NPCs/Needle.md) may be watching Klevane — Dirge doesn't trust anyone completely
- Klevane's brother may be involved or at risk
- Dirge is dangerous in a fight — STR 20, rage, greatsword — and even more dangerous because people follow him willingly
- [Thornwick](../NPCs/Thornwick.md) might be turned if approached correctly — he's the one member of the gang who questions Dirge openly
- [Vael Crookedbow](../NPCs/Vael%20Crookedbow.md) will retreat if the fight turns; she's a survivor, not a zealot
