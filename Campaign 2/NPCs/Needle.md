Needle

[Dirge](Dirge.md)'s eyes and ears. A wood elf scout who moves through the forest like he was born in it — because he was. Barely speaks, communicates mostly through hand signals and pointed looks. The kind of person who'll track you for three days before you even know he's there.

**Race:** Wood elf
**Role:** Scout, [Dirge](Dirge.md)'s forward lookout
**Affiliation:** Dirge's gang

**Stats:**

16 20 14 12 14 8   65HP (10d8+20)  AC16 (studded leather + DEX)  SPD35

Saves: DEX +8, WIS +5
Skills: Perception +8, Stealth +11, Survival +8, Nature +4
Senses: Darkvision 60ft, Passive Perception 18
Languages: Common, Elvish, Sylvan
CR: 5

Fey Ancestry — advantage on saves against being charmed, magic can't put him to sleep
Mask of the Wild — can attempt to hide when only lightly obscured by foliage, rain, snow, mist, or similar
Ambush Master — in the first round of combat, has advantage on attack rolls against any creature that hasn't taken a turn yet. If he hits, the target has disadvantage on initiative if combat is rerolled.

Actions

Multiattack — Needle makes two longbow attacks or two shortsword attacks

Longbow x2  +8, 1D8+5 piercing (150/600)
Shortsword x2  +8, 1D6+5 piercing

Volley (recharge 5-6)
    Needle fires a rapid spread of arrows. Each creature in a 10-foot radius within longbow range must make a DC16 DEX save or take 2D8+5 piercing damage, half on a success.

Bonus Actions

Vanish — Needle can take the Hide action as a bonus action

**Personality:**
- Almost pathologically quiet; other gang members joke that he's part owl
- Loyal to Dirge in the way a hunting dog is loyal — not out of love, but because Dirge is the only person who ever used him well
- Takes no pleasure in killing but doesn't hesitate either
- Has a tell: he whistles a three-note birdcall before an ambush springs. The gang knows to listen for it.

**Role in the Ambush:**
- Positioned ahead of the kill zone, tracking the party's approach
- Responsible for the signal that triggers the attack
- If the party counter-ambushes, Needle is the most likely to detect it — Passive Perception 18
