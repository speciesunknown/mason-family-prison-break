The Ferryman

Operates the only reliable crossing on the River Styx. Ancient beyond reckoning. Speaks slowly and deliberately, as if every word costs him something.

- Appears as a tall, gaunt figure in waterlogged robes
- Face is obscured by a deep hood; only a faint glow where the eyes should be
- His boat is a long, flat-bottomed vessel made of black wood that never rots
- The pole he steers with is carved from a single bone of unknown origin

**Personality:**
- Completely neutral — doesn't care about good, evil, or anything in between
- Treats every passenger the same whether they're a hero, a villain, or a god
- Dry, deadpan sense of humor — has heard every sob story and every threat
- Knows everything that crosses the Styx but volunteers nothing for free

**The Price:**
- Always demands payment, but the currency changes
- Accepts gold, memories, secrets, favors, or years of life
- Loves a good bargain and respects clever negotiation
- Will refuse passage outright if insulted or shortchanged
- Has been known to accept a really good joke in lieu of payment (DC 20 Persuasion)

**Rules of the Crossing:**
- No fighting on the boat — he will capsize it without hesitation
- No touching the water — he will not fish you out
- No asking where he came from — he doesn't remember and doesn't care
